contacts | scan
- Lists known contacts and their navigation status from your current location.
- `scan` is an alias of `contacts`.

Requirements
- Requires `sensord` active (sensors state >= `limited`).
- While in transit, it shows cached known contacts; it does not do a full live sweep unless specific support is present.

Output format
- Header: `=== CONTACTS ===`
- One line per contact, for example:
  `- ECHO-7 Relay Station (station) id=ECHO_7 dist=12.65ly route visited`

How to read each field
- `name (kind)`: display name and node type (`station`, `relay`, `derelict`, etc.).
- `id=...`: internal node identifier (used by commands like `nav` or `dock`).
- `dist=...`: estimated distance from your current position (`ly`, or local fine `km/mi`).
- `route` / `no_route`:
  `route` = there is a known route from your current node to that contact.
  `no_route` = the contact is known, but no route is known from your current node.
- `visited` / `unvisited`: whether you have physically reached that node before.

Important nuance about `route`
- `route` is relative to your current node, not global.
- If you learn route `A -> B` via `uplink`, while at `A` you should see `B` as `route` even if still `unvisited`.
- After traveling to `B`, other entries can switch to `no_route` because the reference origin is now `B`.

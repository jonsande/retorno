route solve <node_id>
- Attempts to resolve/add a known route from your current node to `<node_id>`.
Purpose
- Compute and store missing navigable links from current position to a known target node.

Behavior
- If the route is already known from your current node, no job is queued:
  - It may refine local fine distance and show: `Route refined: fine range fixed for <node_id>`.
  - If nothing can be refined, it reports the route is already known.
- If the route is not known, it queues a `ROUTE_SOLVE` job.
- Job ETA scales with target distance (closer targets finish faster).

Requirements (gating)
- `sensors` state `>= limited`.
- `sensord` service running.
- Target must be known and exist.
- Target must be within sensor range.
- Ship must not be in transit.
- Sufficient power:
  - no critical power state or `brownout`,
  - power quality `Q >= 0.55`.

Common block reasons
- `sensors` degraded/offline.
- `sensord` not running.
- Target out of range.
- Unknown or missing target node.
- Insufficient power quality.

Notes
- `route solve` does not start travel; use `nav <node_id>` to travel.
- Known route status is relative to your current node.

Related commands
- `nav map routes`, `nav map path <node_id>`, `nav <node_id>`, `scan`

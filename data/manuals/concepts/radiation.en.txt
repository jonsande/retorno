SHIP OS — Radiation Model and Exposure Control (Field Copy)
Ref: OS-MAN/ENG/RAD-01

Radiation in RETORNO is persistent systems pressure, not a single-hit event.
It affects ship wear, drone survivability, and long-horizon maintenance cost.

Model layers
- `env_rad`: ambient radiation from current operational space.
- `internal_rad`: radiation ingress into ship internals after hull filtering.
- drone `dose`: accumulated exposure per drone over time.

Environmental source
- Out of transit: current node ambient radiation.
- In transit: average ambient radiation between origin and destination nodes.
- Docking does not create a separate transfer model; docked exposure follows node ambient conditions.
- Radiation follows broad regional patterns plus local anomalies.
  - Region: `bulge` usually runs hotter than `disk`, and `disk` hotter than `halo`.
  - Type: `wreck` nodes (kind `ship`) often read hotter than the surrounding corridor.
  - Floor: even the quietest space still carries a non-zero background dose.

Hull interaction
- Hull integrity (`hull`) is global ship protection state (`0..1`).
- Lower hull integrity increases internal ingress.
- Internal radiation amplifies subsystem degradation through each system `k_rad` factor.

Drone exposure
- Non-docked drones accumulate dose continuously.
- Shield convention (`shield_factor`):
  - `< 1.0`: better shielding (less intake)
  - `= 1.0`: baseline shielding
  - `> 1.0`: worse shielding (more intake)
- Higher dose bands accelerate drone integrity loss.

Bay maintenance interaction
- Docked drones in `drone_bay` are passively decontaminated when bay/distribution support is available.
- Decontamination speed degrades with bay state.

Operational guidance
- Keep drones docked when not actively needed in high-radiation zones.
- Preserve hull integrity to reduce long-term internal-rad pressure.
- Treat radiation as cumulative attrition and schedule maintenance before collapse symptoms appear.

Related commands
- `status`, `drone status [drone_id]`, `diag drone_bay`, `alerts`

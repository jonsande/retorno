SHIP OS — Navigation & Route Knowledge (Field Copy)
Ref: OS-MAN/ENG/NAV-01

Navigation runs on partial knowledge, not a complete galaxy map.
RETORNO combines:
- known nodes/contacts,
- known links/routes,
- local telemetry.

Navigation blocks
- Global topology: `nav map graph [node_id]`.
- Known pathing: `nav map path <node_id>`.
- Direct routes from current position: `nav map routes`.
- Known contacts from current position: `nav map contacts`.
- Known world sectors: `nav map sectors`.
- Galactic situational view (physical layer): `nav map galaxy [sector|local|regional|global]`.
- Transit: `nav <node_id>`, `nav --no-cruise <node_id>`, `nav abort`.

How route knowledge is acquired
- `uplink` on docked relay/station/waystation.
- `route solve` with sufficient sensors + power.
- `scan` for local discovery.
- `intel import` from external logs/fragments.

Relays
- Relays keep and repeat old/partial tables.
- They can expand knowledge, but data freshness is not guaranteed.

Coordinates vs IDs
- `sector`: S{sx}_{sy}_{sz} grid cell from x/y/z.
- `node_id`: operational identifier used by `nav`, `dock`, `ship survey`.

Operational notes
- Knowing a contact is not the same as knowing a route to it.
- `nav map *` depends on `core_os` only (not on `sensors` or `data_core`).
- Region semantics (bulge/disk/halo) are evaluated on the physical galactic layer.
- `nav map galaxy` uses four fixed radii: `10ly`, `100ly`, `10000ly`, `500000ly`.
- Single-hop travel and route publication are guarded by `MAX_ROUTE_HOP_LY` (default `45ly`).
- `ship survey <target>` gives point telemetry, but requires active `sensors` + `data_core`.

Relevant commands
- `nav`, `nav galaxy`, `ship survey`, `scan`, `route solve`, `uplink`, `intel import`, `debug galaxy map <scale>`, `dock`, `undock`.
